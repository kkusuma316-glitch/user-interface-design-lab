Primary Market

Primary Market is the market where companies issue new shares to the public for the first time. Investors buy shares directly from the company, usually through an IPO.

Secondary Market

The Secondary Market is where investors buy and sell existing shares among themselves. Companies do not receive money from these transactions.

Derivatives Market

The Derivatives Market is where financial contracts are traded based on underlying assets like stocks. It includes instruments such as futures and options used for hedging or speculation.
